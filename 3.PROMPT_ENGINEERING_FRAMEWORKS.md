\# PROMPT ENGINEERING FRAMEWORKS



\## Learning Objectives



\- Apply five industry-standard prompt frameworks

\- Select the right framework for different use cases

\- Compare frameworks systematically

\- Build reusable prompt templates using frameworks



\---



\## Introduction



Frameworks give you a repeatable, structured approach to prompt construction. Rather than staring at a blank screen trying to figure out what to type, frameworks give you a scaffold. They ensure you don't forget critical components and help you produce consistently high-quality prompts across different tasks.



\---



\# Framework 1: RTF — Role, Task, Format



\*\*Definition:\*\* The simplest, most widely used framework. Define who the AI is, what it should do, and how to format the output.



\## Structure



```text

ROLE:   \[Who is the AI?]

TASK:   \[What should it do?]

FORMAT: \[How should it present the output?]

```



\*\*Use Case:\*\* Quick task execution, content generation, code writing



\*\*Advantages:\*\* Simple, fast, effective for most tasks



\*\*Limitations:\*\* Lacks context injection and constraint specification for complex tasks



\## Prompt Example



```text

ROLE: You are a senior copywriter specializing in SaaS product marketing.



TASK: Write a LinkedIn post announcing our new AI-powered analytics dashboard feature. The feature helps HR managers reduce time spent on monthly reporting by 70%.



FORMAT:

\- 150–200 words

\- Start with a compelling hook (question or bold statement)

\- Use 2–3 line breaks for readability

\- End with a clear CTA

\- Include 3–5 relevant hashtags

```



\## Expected Output



```text

What if your monthly HR report wrote itself?



Our new AI Analytics Dashboard does exactly that —

automatically pulling data from across your HR stack

and generating ready-to-present reports in minutes.



HR managers at our beta companies are reporting 70%

less time spent on monthly reporting. That's hours

back every week to focus on what actually matters:

your people.



Try it free for 30 days → \[link]



\#HRTech #PeopleAnalytics #AIAtWork #HRLeaders #FutureOfWork

```

\# Framework 2: RISEN — Role, Instructions, Steps, End Goal, Narrowing



\*\*Definition:\*\* A comprehensive framework that adds step-by-step instructions and clearly defines the end goal and scope constraints.



\## Structure



```text

ROLE:         \[Who is the AI?]

INSTRUCTIONS: \[Detailed guidance on how to approach the task]

STEPS:        \[Numbered sequence of what to do]

END GOAL:     \[What does success look like?]

NARROWING:    \[What to exclude / scope limits]

```



\*\*Use Case:\*\* Complex analysis, multi-step research tasks, detailed reports



\*\*Advantages:\*\* Highly structured; excellent for complex workflows; reduces ambiguity



\*\*Limitations:\*\* More complex to write; overkill for simple tasks



\## Prompt Example



```text

ROLE: You are a UX research analyst with expertise in B2B software.



INSTRUCTIONS: Conduct a comprehensive analysis of the provided user interview transcripts. Look for patterns, pain points, and unmet needs.



STEPS:

1\. Read all three transcripts completely

2\. Extract all explicit pain points mentioned

3\. Identify implicit needs (what users struggle with but didn't directly name)

4\. Group findings into themes

5\. Prioritize themes by frequency and severity

6\. Suggest 3 product improvements backed by specific quotes



END GOAL: A research synthesis report that a product team can use to make prioritization decisions for Q3 roadmap planning.



NARROWING: Focus only on the onboarding experience. Ignore feedback about pricing, support, or integrations. Maximum 500 words.



\[TRANSCRIPTS]: \[paste transcripts]

```

\# Framework 3: CARE — Context, Action, Result, Example



\*\*Definition:\*\* Focuses on grounding the prompt with context, specifying the action, defining the desired result, and providing an example to guide the output.



\## Structure



```text

CONTEXT: \[Background information]

ACTION:  \[What to do]

RESULT:  \[What the output should achieve]

EXAMPLE: \[A sample of desired output]

```



\*\*Use Case:\*\* Content generation with specific tone/style requirements; tutoring and explanation tasks



\## Prompt Example



```text

CONTEXT: I'm a first-time entrepreneur preparing to pitch a Series A investor next week. My startup is an AI-powered legal contract review tool for SMBs. I've never pitched to investors before.



ACTION: Critique the following pitch opening and rewrite it to be more compelling and investor-ready.



My current opening: "We built a tool that uses AI to review contracts and it can save businesses money."



RESULT: A rewritten pitch opening that is specific, quantified, communicates the problem clearly, and hooks the investor in under 30 seconds.



EXAMPLE: "American SMBs spend $1.2B annually reviewing legal contracts they don't understand — and 73% sign agreements with unfavorable clauses. ContractAI identifies and flags those clauses in 4 minutes, at 10% of the cost of a lawyer."

```

\# Framework 4: COAST — Context, Objective, Action, Style, Tone



\*\*Definition:\*\* Particularly effective for creative and marketing content where style and tone are as important as the information itself.



\## Structure



```text

CONTEXT:  \[Situation and background]

OBJECTIVE:\[Goal of the content]

ACTION:   \[Specific task]

STYLE:    \[Writing style / approach]

TONE:     \[Emotional register / voice]

```



\*\*Use Case:\*\* Marketing copy, brand communication, customer emails, blog posts



\## Prompt Example



```text

CONTEXT: Our fintech startup just raised $10M Series A. We're a team of 25 building instant cross-border payment infrastructure for African businesses. Most of our customers are small exporters.



OBJECTIVE: Celebrate the milestone with our customers, strengthen brand loyalty, and subtly communicate that we're a serious, growing company they can trust long-term.



ACTION: Write a customer announcement email about our funding round.



STYLE: Warm and personal, not corporate. Use "we" and "you." Short paragraphs. Tell a brief story about why this matters for our customers. Avoid buzzwords like "synergy" or "leverage."



TONE: Grateful, excited, grounded. Not boastful. Make customers feel like they're part of the journey.

```



\---



\# Framework 5: STAR — Situation, Task, Action, Result



\*\*Definition:\*\* Borrowed from behavioral interview technique, STAR is excellent for case studies, reports, and structured storytelling.



\## Structure



```text

SITUATION: \[What was happening?]

TASK:      \[What needed to be done?]

ACTION:    \[What was done and how?]

RESULT:    \[What was the outcome?]

```



\*\*Use Case:\*\* Case studies, impact reports, performance reviews, portfolio write-ups



\---



\# Framework Comparison Table



| Framework | Best For | Complexity | Key Strength |

|------------|-----------|------------|---------------|

| RTF | Quick tasks | Low | Speed |

| RISEN | Complex tasks | High | Structure |

| CARE | Explanation | Medium | Example-guided |

| COAST | Creative/Mktg | Medium | Voice \& tone |

| STAR | Case studies | Medium | Storytelling |

\# Exercise — Framework Selection



\## Scenario



You work at a digital marketing agency. Choose the most appropriate framework and write the complete prompt for each:



1\. Write a product description for an eco-friendly water bottle

2\. Analyze a 20-page market research report and extract key insights

3\. Write a case study about how you helped a client increase revenue by 40%



\## Answers



1\. \*\*COAST\*\* — creative content requiring specific tone and style  

2\. \*\*RISEN\*\* — complex analysis with clear steps and scope  

3\. \*\*STAR\*\* — structured storytelling for case study format
