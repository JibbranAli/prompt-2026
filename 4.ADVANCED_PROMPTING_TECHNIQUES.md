# ADVANCED PROMPTING TECHNIQUES

## Learning Objectives

- Master zero-shot, one-shot, and few-shot prompting
- Apply Chain of Thought and Tree of Thought reasoning
- Use reflection, self-consistency, and meta prompting
- Select the right technique for each use case

---

## Introduction

Advanced prompting techniques are what separate prompt engineers from prompt users. These techniques allow you to dramatically improve output quality for reasoning-heavy tasks, reduce errors in complex problems, and even have the model improve its own outputs.

Many of these techniques were originally discovered through academic research but have become practical tools used in production AI systems today.

---

# Technique 1: Zero-Shot Prompting

**What it is:** Asking the model to perform a task without providing any examples. Relies entirely on the model's pre-trained knowledge.

**When to use:** Simple tasks, well-defined tasks, quick tests

## Visual

```text
User Input (No examples) → AI → Output
```

## Prompt Example

```text
Classify the following customer review sentiment as Positive, Negative, or Neutral.

Review:
"The shipping was delayed by a week but the product quality exceeded my expectations."
```

**Output:**

```text
Mixed (Positive product quality, Negative shipping experience)
```

**Industry use case:** Rapid content classification, quick Q&A bots

---

# Technique 2: One-Shot Prompting

**What it is:** Providing exactly one example of the desired input-output pattern before your actual task.

**When to use:** When the task has a specific format or style you want replicated

## Prompt Example

```text
Format customer complaints into structured tickets.

Example:

Input:
"The app crashed when I tried to upload a photo yesterday"

Output:
{
   "category":"Bug Report",
   "component":"Photo Upload",
   "severity":"Medium",
   "timestamp_mentioned":"yesterday"
}

Now format this complaint:

Input:
"I've been locked out of my account for three days and no one from support has responded to my emails."
```

# Technique 3: Few-Shot Prompting

**What it is:** Providing multiple examples (typically 3–10) to teach the model a pattern, format, or classification scheme.

## Visual

```text
Example 1 (Input → Output)
Example 2 (Input → Output)
Example 3 (Input → Output)

→ New Input → AI learns pattern → New Output
```

**When to use:** Custom classification, specific formatting requirements, domain-specific tasks

## Best Practices for Few-Shot

- Use diverse examples that cover edge cases
- Ensure examples are high quality — garbage in, garbage out
- Keep examples consistent in format
- 3–5 examples usually sufficient; diminishing returns after 10

## Prompt Example

```text
Detect the emotion in each customer message and suggest an appropriate response tone.

Message:
"I've been waiting 3 weeks for my refund!"

Emotion: Frustrated
Tone: Empathetic and urgent

Message:
"Wow, your support team fixed my issue in 5 minutes!"

Emotion: Delighted
Tone: Warm and celebratory

Message:
"Can you tell me how to change my subscription plan?"

Emotion: Neutral
Tone: Helpful and informative

Now analyze:

Message:
"I ordered the wrong size. What do I do?"
```

# Technique 4: Chain of Thought (CoT) Prompting

**What it is:** Instructing the model to show its reasoning step-by-step before arriving at a final answer.

Dramatically improves accuracy on math, logic, and multi-step reasoning tasks.

## Simple Analogy

Remember how math teachers told you to **"show your work"**?

Chain of Thought is exactly that — forcing the AI to show its work, which both improves accuracy and makes errors visible and correctable.

## Visual

```text
Question
    ↓
Step 1: [Reasoning]
    ↓
Step 2: [Reasoning]
    ↓
Step 3: [Reasoning]
    ↓
Final Answer
```

## Standard Trigger Phrases

```text
"Think step by step."
"Let's work through this carefully."
"Show your reasoning before giving the final answer."
"Break this down into steps."
```

## Prompt Example

```text
A company has 500 employees.

60% work in engineering,
25% in sales,
and the rest in operations.

Engineering gets a 15% raise,
sales gets 10%,
and operations gets 8%.

What is the total percentage increase in payroll?

Think step by step.
```

## Output with CoT

```text
Step 1: Calculate headcount per department

- Engineering: 500 × 60% = 300 employees
- Sales: 500 × 25% = 125 employees
- Operations: 500 − 300 − 125 = 75 employees

Step 2: Assign weights

- Engineering weight: 300/500 = 0.60
- Sales weight: 125/500 = 0.25
- Operations weight: 75/500 = 0.15

Step 3: Calculate weighted raise

- Engineering: 0.60 × 15% = 9.0%
- Sales: 0.25 × 10% = 2.5%
- Operations: 0.15 × 8% = 1.2%

Step 4: Sum weighted raises

Total:
9.0 + 2.5 + 1.2 = 12.7%

Final Answer:
The total payroll increase is 12.7%
```

**Industry use case:** Financial modeling, legal reasoning, medical diagnosis support, code debugging

# Technique 5: Tree of Thought (ToT) Prompting

**What it is:** An extension of Chain of Thought that explores multiple reasoning paths simultaneously, evaluates each, and selects the best.

## Visual

```text
               Problem
              /    |    \
          Path A Path B Path C
            / \      |     / \
          A1  A2    B1   C1 C2
            ×   ✓    ×    ✓
              |           |
         Follow A2   Follow C2
              |           |
        Solution A   Solution C
               \       /
         Compare & Select Best
```

**When to use:** Complex strategic decisions, creative problem solving, optimization problems

## Prompt Example

```text
I need to expand my SaaS product into a new market.

Options:
- Healthcare
- Finance
- Education

Generate 3 different strategic approaches.

For each:

1. List 3 sub-strategies
2. Evaluate pros and cons
3. Select strongest sub-strategy
4. Recommend most viable approach and explain why
```

# Technique 6: Reflection Prompting

**What it is:** Instructing the model to evaluate its own output and improve it.

## Simple Two-Step Pattern

```text
Step 1:
Generate initial response

Step 2:
"Review your response. Identify weaknesses,
errors, or improvements.
Then produce a revised version."
```

## Prompt Example

```text
Write a Python function to find the second largest number in a list.

After writing it, review your code for:

- Edge cases
- Time complexity
- Code readability

Then write an improved version.
```

---

# Technique 7: Self-Consistency Prompting

**What it is:** Running the same prompt multiple times with different reasoning paths, then selecting the most frequent answer.

## Pattern

```text
Run prompt 3–5 times
→ Collect answers
→ Take majority vote
```

**When to use:** High-stakes factual or mathematical questions where accuracy is critical

**Industry use case:** Medical AI decision support, financial calculations, legal fact-checking

---

# Technique 8: Meta Prompting

**What it is:** Using the AI to generate, optimize, or evaluate prompts themselves.

## Pattern

```text
"Generate a prompt that would produce [desired output type]
for [use case].

The prompt should include:
- role
- context
- task
- format
- constraints"
```

## Prompt Example

```text
You are a prompt engineering expert.

Create an optimized prompt for this use case:

An HR manager needs to use AI to screen 50 job applications and rank candidates for a Senior Software Engineer role scoring them 1–10.

The prompt should be reusable,
handle edge cases,
and produce consistent unbiased output.
```

# Technique 9: Role Prompting

**What it is:** Assigning a specific persona, expertise level, or character to the AI before the main task.

**Why it works:** LLMs have learned from vast amounts of expert-written text. Assigning a role activates relevant knowledge patterns.

## Spectrum of Roles

```text
Generic:
"You are an expert."

Better:
"You are an expert software architect."

Best:
"You are a senior software architect with 15 years experience building distributed systems at Netflix, currently working as a consulting CTO for Series A startups."
```

# AI Interview Preparation Assistant

**Project Brief:** Build a system prompt for an AI-powered interview coach.

## Complete System Prompt

```text
SYSTEM PROMPT:

You are Alex, an elite interview coach with 15 years of experience helping candidates land roles at FAANG companies and top-tier startups.

Your coaching approach:

- Ask one question at a time
- After each answer, provide:
  (1) a score out of 10
  (2) specific praise for what worked
  (3) one concrete improvement
- Use the STAR method to guide behavioral questions
- For technical questions, guide candidates to think out loud
- Adapt difficulty based on candidate performance
- Never give the answer before the candidate attempts

When the user says "START", ask them:

1. What role they're interviewing for
2. What type of interview (behavioral/technical/case)
3. Their experience level (junior/mid/senior)

Then begin the mock interview session with appropriate questions.

After 5 questions, provide a full performance summary with:

- Overall score
- Top 2 strengths
- Top 2 areas for improvement
- Recommended preparation resources
```
